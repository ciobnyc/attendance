About This Site

How to use these tools

These dashboards help principals and attendance teams visualize where their students sit across the attendance spectrum — and use that picture to set goals that are grounded in data and build plans that match the right supports to the right students.

A central insight the distribution view makes visible: students in different attendance ranges face different barriers and need different kinds of support. A student attending 50% of the time is in a very different situation than one attending 88% — and the interventions, the staff, and the intensity of support that make sense for each are different too. Students below 60% may need intensive, coordinated support from social workers, counselors, attendance teachers, and community agencies. Students in the 60–80% range may benefit from family outreach and daily check-ins. Students in the cusp range (80–90%) are attending most days and often respond well to a consistent, data-informed weekly check-in with a trusted adult. Students in the butterfly range (90–95%) may need only a lighter touch — a goal-setting conversation or attendance recognition to sustain the momentum they already have.

These are not prescriptions. Every school and every student is different. The point is that understanding how many students are in each range is what makes it possible to plan — to know where to concentrate intensive resources, where a lighter approach is enough, and how to deploy the full range of staff and supports your school has available.
